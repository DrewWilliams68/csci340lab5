The story begins!

A long time ago, there was a kingdom named Chess. A quaint little place ruled by a kind King and Queen. These two ruled over chess for years, growing closer together everyday. On the young rulers' anniversary, the Queen gifted the King a blue wool scarf that she made. The King charished this scarf so much that it never left his side. With tensions of the bordering kingdom growing stronger, the King was forced to command his army and go on many tiring journeys. The only thing that got him through these rough times was this, now weathered, scarf. It was constant reminder of the love he shared with his beloved Queen. Finally, after many months of this conflict, the two lands made a peace treaty. Content, the King lead his tired soldiers home to their kingdom. After arriving back home, they had a huge banquet to celebrate their triumphant return! With the feeling of peace restored, the King, Queen, and citizens of chess, retired back to their homes. But this peace would soon be short lived... In the late of hours of the night, chaos erupted. Awoken by the uproar, the King and Queen realized the bordering kingdom was invading. The peace treaty was apparenty a ruse and the bordering kingdom is now attacking the kingdom. In response to this betrayal, the King and Queen gathered their remaining loyal subjects which include:

  • Two Bishops
  • Two Knights
  • Two Rooks
  • Eight Pawns
With these forces, the chances of defeating the rival army was slim, but nevertheless they entered the battle. Under the direction of their King, Chess's forces managed to hold off the opposing army for a short while. Eventually, the forces of Chess were overwhelmed and all that remained were a few pawns and the King and Queen. The King, intent on escaping with his bride, took out an opposing Knight and bishop while leading the Queen to safety. The Queen however, knew that the only chance winning against the opposing King, was to sacrifice herself and let one of her pawns advance. Knowing the King would never agree to this, she stopped running and managed to take the last remaining Rook and pawns before meeting her end to the opposing King. The King, distraught, ran to his wife's corpse. Unable to go on, the King was unable to capture the opposing King, so it ended in a stale mate. With his love gone, his palace burned to the ground, and most of his kingdom in shambles, he had to keep moving forward and manage this catastrophe. By morning the kingdom started showing signs of recovery. After the flames died down, the King returned to his crumbled castle, looking for any remnant of his late Queen. In the rubble of what used to be his bedroom, he found his, now singed, scarf. Unable to handle his grief, he found a semi flat pile of rubble, which he sat and reflected for hours. All that remained of his Queen was this scarf and the lingering regret of being fooled by his enemy.

The End
